How to work together

Engagement models for technical momentum.

Structured ways to bring Chatterguru into proposal, architecture, prototype, integration, software, analytics, and review work without adding process weight.

Start with the shape of the problem.

These public-facing models make the first conversation concrete while leaving room for the customer environment, contracting path, and review constraints.

01

Technical Review Sprint

Fast architecture, MBSE, software, cyber, analytics, or radar/RF review that turns scattered context into prioritized findings and next actions.

02

Prototype Build

Small, working software or modeling effort designed to answer a decision question, demonstrate feasibility, or support a proposal narrative.

03

Delivery Support

Part-time or embedded technical leadership for requirements, integration, DevSecOps, model cleanup, data workflows, and stakeholder-ready artifacts.

Typical outputs.

  • Architecture notes, model views, assumptions, and risk register updates.
  • Prototype code, demo workflows, synthetic data, and visualization artifacts.
  • Proposal language, capability statement input, and review-ready diagrams.
  • Integration, test, verification, and DevSecOps next-step recommendations.

Best first fit

Use the review sprint when the work is blurry, the prototype build when the question needs evidence, and delivery support when a team needs steady engineering bandwidth.

Discuss an engagement

A simple path from question to artifact.

1Clarify the decision, audience, constraints, and public-release boundaries.
2Map the technical thread across systems, software, data, cyber, and mission context.
3Build the smallest useful artifact: model view, prototype, assessment, or proposal-ready package.
4Leave the customer with documented assumptions, recommended next steps, and reusable source material.
