Case study templates that stay release-safe.

Reusable page structures for cleared, public examples later. These are templates only and should not be filled with client, program, system, or sensitive details without review.

Recommended public case study categories.

Each template keeps the story useful for visitors while protecting sensitive implementation details.

Template

Architecture and MBSE Recovery

Show how a fragmented technical effort became easier to reason about through requirements cleanup, model views, interfaces, and verification planning.

Template

Advanced Sensing Prototype

Describe a synthetic modeling or visualization prototype at a high level, focusing on workflow, assumptions, and decision support rather than system performance.

Template

Secure Software Modernization

Frame containerization, automation, DevSecOps, API integration, or embedded software improvements around speed, repeatability, and maintainability.

Template

Analytics Decision Support

Present an AI, data science, optimization, forecasting, or visualization effort as a decision aid with clear inputs, outputs, and limits.

Template

Proposal Technical Story

Explain how complex capabilities were organized into a credible architecture, risk, schedule, and delivery narrative for a pursuit team.

Template

Integration and Test Planning

Highlight how requirements, interfaces, test events, and evidence collection were turned into a practical path toward verification.

Safe case study structure.

ProblemDescribe the business or engineering friction in generic terms.
ApproachExplain the methods, artifacts, and collaboration model without exposing sensitive parameters.
OutcomeUse qualitative or approved quantitative impact that has been cleared for public use.
BoundaryState what was intentionally omitted, anonymized, or generalized.

Public-release checklist

  • No customer-identifying detail without approval.
  • No real system parameters, threat data, or restricted implementation detail.
  • No export-controlled or proprietary artifacts.
  • No operational claims that cannot be publicly supported.
