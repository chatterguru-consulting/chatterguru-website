## Why These Pages

- Home: replaces the placeholder with a clear company promise and immediate navigation.
- Capabilities: gives government, contractor, and technical-industry visitors a quick scan of the full offering.
- Systems and MBSE: highlights the founder's core systems-engineering credibility and makes proposal/architecture work easy to understand.
- Radar/RF: gives advanced sensing, RF, satellite, radar, and EW work a careful public-facing page without exposing sensitive details.
- Software and Cyber: groups secure software, DevSecOps, embedded software, microservices, and cybersecurity.
- AI and Data: gives machine learning, analytics, optimization, and visualization a dedicated entry point.
- Engagement Models: helps prospects understand review sprints, prototypes, and delivery-support options.
- Case Study Templates: optional hidden/resource page for future cleared public proof points.
- Team: builds trust quickly by showing the multidisciplinary bench.
- Contact: makes it easy to start a conversation around proposal support, prototypes, or technical reviews.

## Public Website Tone

- Keep language credible, precise, and executive-readable.
- Avoid over-specific claims about defense systems, real threat systems, or restricted implementation detail.
- Use the brochure's strengths, but turn dense capability lists into short, visitor-centered page sections.
- Add cleared case studies later only after a public-release review.
